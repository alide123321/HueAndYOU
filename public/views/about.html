About Hue & YOU

Hue & YOU is a color palette generation tool designed to help users create and explore color harmonies for interface and visual design. The system allows users to generate palettes, preview them, and work with structured color relationships in a more intentional way.

How it works

Hue & YOU generates color palettes based on a base color and a selected harmony strategy. Each setting on the generator page influences how your palette is created.

  • Base Color: The starting color used to generate the palette.
  • Harmony Type: Defines how colors are spaced (Analogous, Triadic, Tetradic, etc.).
  • Number of Swatches: Controls how many colors appear in the palette.
  • Generate: Produces multiple palette variations based on your selections.
  • Preview & Edit: View palettes in context and refine individual colors in the editor.

Generated Variants

Hue & YOU can generate multiple palette variants from the same starting color and harmony type. These variants help users explore different interpretations of the same palette structure rather than being limited to a single output.

Variants may differ through adjustments such as:

  • Lightness: Produces lighter or darker palette versions
  • Chroma: Adjusts how vivid or muted the colors appear
  • Hue Spacing: Changes the distance between colors in the harmony

In some cases, the number of generated palettes may vary depending on the selected settings and the type of harmony being used. This is because different harmony strategies and adjustments can produce different ranges of useful palette outputs.

Editing and Preview

After generating a palette, users can move to the editor to refine and explore their colors in more detail.

  • Adjust Swatches: Modify individual colors within a palette to better fit your needs.
  • Rename Palettes: Organize and identify palettes by giving them meaningful names.
  • Preview Panel: View your palette applied to a sample interface to evaluate contrast and usability.

The preview helps users understand how colors will appear in real-world UI scenarios, making it easier to create palettes that are both visually appealing and accessible.

Accessibility and WCAG

Accessibility is an important part of color design. Hue & YOU encourages users to consider contrast, readability, and usability when creating palettes. The Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) provide standards for making digital content more accessible, especially for users with visual impairments.
